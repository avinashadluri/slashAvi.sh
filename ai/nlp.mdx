> _I'm a practitioner, not a researcher. These notes reflect applied use on real products, not a survey of NLP methods._

Language is central to everything I build — technical documentation, Telugu creative content, preventive thinking tools. Here's how I actually work with it.

---

## What I Actually Need to Understand

- **When to use RAG vs fine-tuning vs prompting** — RAG (retrieval-augmented generation) is the right first choice for knowledge-intensive tasks where the source content changes frequently. Fine-tuning wins when the task pattern is highly specific and stable. Prompting wins for everything else.
- **Chunking strategy matters more than model choice** — For most RAG systems I've built, the biggest quality improvement came from better chunking, not a bigger model. Chunk at semantic boundaries, not character limits.
- **Evaluation is the hard part** — BLEU/ROUGE are fast to compute but poorly correlated with what users actually value. I use LLM-as-judge for most evaluation now, with a human review pass on a random sample.

## The Tools I Use and Why

| Task | Tool | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Embeddings | OpenAI text-embedding-3-small | Good quality-cost ratio for English; lower cost than large |
| Reranking | Cohere Rerank | Significant retrieval quality improvement, worth the latency |
| Vector storage | Supabase pgvector | Good enough for <1M vectors; avoids running a separate service |
| Large-scale vectors | Pinecone | When Supabase pgvector hits performance limits |
| RAG orchestration | Vercel AI SDK | Native streaming; integrates with my Next.js frontend |
| Audio → text | Whisper | Best accuracy on bilingual (Telugu + English) content |
| Bilingual NLP | Custom normalizer + HuggingFace | Telugu isn't well-covered by off-the-shelf pipelines |

## One Concrete Example: Thinki.sh Knowledge Retrieval

Thinki.sh's AI coaching layer needs to retrieve relevant framework content based on what a user describes. The challenge: the user's language is rarely aligned with how the frameworks are named.

A user says: "I keep being surprised when my projects go over budget."
The relevant framework: Pre-Mortem + Second-Order Thinking.

Keyword search fails here. Semantic search alone misses context. The solution I settled on:

1. **Embed** the user's problem statement
2. **Retrieve** top-10 candidate chunks from the framework knowledge base (pgvector cosine similarity)
3. **Rerank** with Cohere Rerank — drops to top 3 chunks most contextually relevant to the specific situation described
4. **Generate** the coaching response with a prompt that includes the retrieved chunks and explicit instructions to stay grounded in them

The reranking step alone improved response relevance (measured by user "this was helpful" ratings) from 62% to 81%. It added 300ms latency. Worth it.

## The Bilingual Pipeline (English + Telugu)

NLP tooling is built almost entirely around English. Using it for Telugu requires workarounds:

- **Tokenization**: Standard tokenizers mangle Telugu script. I use a custom normalizer before passing to most models.
- **Embeddings**: OpenAI's embeddings handle Telugu reasonably well for semantic similarity. They're weaker for fine-grained Telugu sentiment.
- **Sentiment analysis**: Off-the-shelf models fail on bilingual Telugu-English content. I built a fine-tuned classifier on ~2,000 labeled examples from Nishabdham content and user feedback.
- **Translation quality evaluation**: Automated BLEU scores are almost meaningless for English-Telugu because the sentence structures are so different. I use human review for anything culturally significant.

The honest position: Telugu NLP at production quality requires more custom work than I expected. It's getting better — recent multilingual models are significantly improved — but "it handles Telugu" in a model card often means "it doesn't crash on Telugu," not "it handles Telugu well."

---

## What I Learned the Hard Way

**1. Retrieval quality, not model quality, is usually the bottleneck.** The first RAG system I built had excellent embeddings and a weak retrieval strategy. Switching models didn't help. Fixing the chunking and adding reranking did.

**2. Users don't ask questions the way your documents are structured.** Design your chunking around how people will ask, not around how you've organized the content. This often means chunking at the FAQ level, not the section level.

**3. Latency compounds.** Embed + retrieve + rerank + generate can easily hit 3-4 seconds end-to-end. For a chat interface, this is acceptable. For anything that needs to feel real-time, you need streaming at minimum, and probably prefetching of likely next queries.
